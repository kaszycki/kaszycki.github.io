Course Overview

This is an introductory course concerning the workings of the government of the United States of America. Students will gain a stronger understanding of the structure and function of governmental institutions, civil society, and the policy process.

Textbooks

The following textbooks are required:
- Bullock, Charles S., and Ronald K. Gaddie. Georgia Politics in a State of Change, Fourth Custom Edition for the University of Georgia.
- Heffner, Richard D. and Alexander Heffner, A Documentary History of the United States Signet, 2013
- Hofstadter, Richard, and Christopher Lasch. The American political tradition and the men who made it. Vintage, 1989.
- Issenberg, Sasha. The victory lab: The secret science of winning campaigns. Broadway Books, 2012.
- Patashnik, Eric M. Reforms at Risk: What Happens After Major Policy Changes Are Enacted: What Happens After Major Policy Changes Are Enacted. Princeton University Press, 2014.

Course Requirements

Grades will be determined per the following breakdown:
Tests 15% each
There will be three tests during the semester, at scheduled times. Each will count for 15% of your final grade. The tests will primarily be short answer.
Quizzes 25%
There will be roughly weekly quizzes (10 points each), Your grade for these quizzes will be out of (n-1)x10 points.
Citizenship Test 10%
I will give you a citizenship test consisting of actual questions on the US citizenship test given to immigrants wanting to gain citizenship in the United States. A passing score is 60%. If you get this, you get the 10 points. If you fail it, you get 0. You’ll have up to two tries.
Participation 20%
This is a (relatively) small section of American Government; as such, your participation is desired. Merely showing up (attendance) is a necessary but not sufficient condition for gaining these points.

Final grades will be assigned as follow: 100-93 = A, 90-92 = A, 87-89 = B+, 83-86 = B, 80-82 = B,77-79 = C+, 73-76 = C, 70-72 = C, 60-69 = D, and 60-0 = F.
There are no extra credit assignments, nor is there a curve in this course.
